## 5. Exact weekly calendar

### 5.1 Signal and execution timestamps

A scheduled weekly execution timestamp is a candle open whose UTC weekday is Monday and whose UTC time is `00:00:00`.

For each scheduled execution open:

- signal timestamp is exactly the immediately preceding completed Sunday `20:00:00 UTC` candle;
- all signal inputs end at that signal timestamp;
- the execution uses the next Monday `00:00:00 UTC` open;
- the signal may use the required 42-bar history before an independent window starts, but PnL begins only at the window start.

The first scheduled execution in each window is therefore:

- S1: `2024-01-01T00:00:00Z`, using signal bar `2023-12-31T20:00:00Z`;
- S2: `2024-04-01T00:00:00Z`, using signal bar `2024-03-31T20:00:00Z`;
- S3: `2024-07-01T00:00:00Z`, using signal bar `2024-06-30T20:00:00Z`.

Each window remains economically independent even though its first signal may use earlier development candles as startup history.

## 6. Exact weekly returns for DSR

Deflated Sharpe calculations use only full, non-overlapping Monday-to-Monday weeks that lie completely inside an independent window.

Full weekly observation counts:

- S1: `13` full weeks;
- S2: `13` full weeks;
- S3: `13` full weeks;
- concatenated DSR sample count: exactly `39`.

The partial terminal S3 interval from `2024-09-30T00:00:00Z` to the final `2024-09-30T20:00:00Z` close:

- contributes to economic PnL, drawdown, turnover, and terminal liquidation;
- is excluded from the DSR weekly sample because it is not a full 42-bar week.

For each full week:

`weekly_net_return = equity_at_next_monday_open_before_that_open_rebalance / equity_at_current_monday_open_after_that_open_rebalance - 1`

Conventions:

- the current Monday rebalance fee is reflected in the starting post-rebalance equity;
- the next Monday mark is measured before the next rebalance fee;
- cash weeks are included with their actual return, normally zero;
- no return observation crosses an independent window boundary;
- window-end liquidation cost is not inserted into a full-week DSR observation unless the liquidation occurs exactly at that full week's ending mark;
- all economic costs remain included in standard economic metrics regardless of DSR inclusion.

The production and reference implementations must retain the exact `39` observation values for every policy at expected cost.

## 7. Exact cross-sectional ranks

For a numeric signal:

1. sort assets by signal descending;
2. resolve equal numeric values by lexical pair ascending;
3. assign integer rank `8` to the first asset, `7` to the second, continuing to `1` for the eighth.

No average rank is used for ties.

For `C4ACompositeMomentumTopTwo`:

`composite_score = (weekly_return_rank + high_proximity_rank) / 2`

Final composite selection key:

1. composite score, descending;
2. weekly return, descending;
3. high proximity, descending;
4. pair string, ascending.

Positive-return eligibility is applied before selecting the final top two, but rank values are computed across all eight selected-universe assets.

## 8. Exact breadth and cash state

`breadth = positive_weekly_return_count / 8`

Risk-on requires both:

- positive count at least `4`; and
- at least two eligible assets after the policy's positive-return restriction.

Because the denominator is fixed at eight, `breadth >= 0.50` is exactly equivalent to positive count at least four.

When risk-on is false:

- target asset set is empty;
- target cash weight is `1.0`;
- all existing positions are liquidated at the scheduled open;
- no entry signal is queued for a later week.

## 9. Rebalance-equation numerics

Let:

- `E_before` be gross equity at the scheduled open before fees;
- `v_i` be each selected-universe asset's current marked value, including zero values;
- `w_i` be its frozen target weight, either `0`, `0.45`, or another exact target permitted by the policy contract;
- `f` be the one-side cost rate.

Define:

`g(E) = E + f * sum(abs(w_i * E - v_i)) - E_before`

The desired post-cost equity is the unique root of `g(E) = 0` on `[0, E_before]`.

Bisection rules:

1. lower bound `lo = 0`;
2. upper bound `hi = E_before`;
3. require `g(lo) <= 0` and `g(hi) >= 0` within `1e-12`;
4. midpoint is `(lo + hi) / 2`;
5. if `g(mid) > 0`, replace `hi`; otherwise replace `lo`;
6. stop when `abs(g(mid)) <= 1e-12` or interval width is at most `1e-12` USDT;
7. maximum `200` iterations;
8. failure to converge is `EVIDENCE_FAILURE`.

After solving:

- target asset value is `w_i * E_after`;
- trade notional is `target_value_i - v_i`;
- fee is `f * sum(abs(trade_notional_i))`;
- target quantity is `target_value_i / open_price_i`;
- cash is `E_after - sum(target_value_i)`.

Validation tolerances:

- absolute accounting identity error: at most `1e-9` USDT;
- negative cash or quantity tolerance: none; values below zero are invalid, not clipped;
- all prices, quantities, notionals, fees, and equity values must be finite.

## 10. Exact Sharpe conventions

### 10.1 Four-hour economic Sharpe

For standard aggregate economic Sharpe:

- concatenate independent-window four-hour marked-to-market returns in S1, S2, S3 order;
- do not create a return across window boundaries;
- use arithmetic sample mean;
- use sample standard deviation with `ddof=1`;
- annualization factor is `sqrt(6 * 365)`;
- if standard deviation is zero and mean is zero, Sharpe is `0`;
- if standard deviation is zero and mean is nonzero, the evidence run fails.

### 10.2 Weekly trial Sharpe

For each policy's 39 DSR observations:

- use arithmetic sample mean;
- use sample standard deviation with `ddof=1`;
- annualization factor is `sqrt(52)`;
- if standard deviation is zero and mean is zero, weekly Sharpe is `0` and that policy's DSR is `0`;
- if standard deviation is zero and mean is nonzero, the evidence run fails.

The three weekly policy Sharpes form the complete multiple-testing trial set.

## 11. Exact Deflated Sharpe numerics

Frozen numerical definitions:

- `N = 3`;
- Euler-Mascheroni constant: `0.5772156649015329`;
- Euler's number: the platform's correctly rounded `exp(1)`;
- normal CDF and inverse CDF: SciPy `scipy.stats.norm.cdf` and `scipy.stats.norm.ppf` in production;
- reference implementation must use an independently coded or separately imported equivalent and match to tolerance.

Across the three weekly policy Sharpes:

- `sigma_SR` uses sample standard deviation with `ddof=1`;
- if all three Sharpes are exactly equal, set `SR_star = 0`;
- otherwise calculate `SR_star` exactly as frozen in the parent contract.

For each policy's 39 weekly returns:

- skewness uses bias-corrected sample skew: SciPy `skew(..., bias=False)`;
- kurtosis uses bias-corrected ordinary kurtosis: SciPy `kurtosis(..., fisher=False, bias=False)`;
- denominator radicand is `1 - skew * SR + ((kurtosis - 1) / 4) * SR^2`;
- a non-finite or non-positive radicand is `EVIDENCE_FAILURE`;
- calculate the probability with the frozen DSR equation;
- the final probability must be finite and in `[0, 1]`.

Comparison tolerance between production and reference DSR components:

- absolute tolerance `1e-10`;
- relative tolerance `1e-10`.

The artifact must retain, for each policy:

- the 39 weekly returns;
- weekly mean and sample standard deviation;
- annualized weekly Sharpe;
- skewness;
- ordinary kurtosis;
- `sigma_SR`;
- `SR_star`;
- denominator radicand;
- z-score passed to the normal CDF;
- final DSR probability;
- eligibility pass/fail.

## 12. Multiple-testing trial discipline

Exactly three economic policy trials exist in C4A. The trial count may not exclude a weak policy, a policy that remains in cash, or a policy that fails before final ranking after producing valid economic results.

The following are forbidden because they would increase the effective trial count without preregistration:

- testing another lookback;
- testing another breadth threshold;
- testing top one, top three, or another position count;
- testing another cash weight;
- testing daily or alternate-week schedules;
- testing another candidate pool or top-universe size;
- testing a different composite formula;
- testing a policy only to decide whether to include it in the reported trial set.

Implementation bugs that prevent a valid economic result are evidence failures and may be corrected only before any valid economic output is observed, with the incident and recovery chain retained. After a valid economic output is produced, no workflow recovery may change economic semantics.

- any access to C4B or holdout data;
- any private API, order-book, derivative, leverage, or live path.
